This is the fallacy of using aggregate data to predict individual behavior - and people fall for it constantly.

### association / Section 3.7

This connects to something from earlier chapters - the 'beauty as communication' insight. pSMV is essentially about how well your signals are decoded by a particular receiver. A man with high nSMV (wealth, status) who fails at pSMV is someone whose signals aren't being received or correctly interpreted. It's the encoder-receiver problem all over again.

### highlight / Section 3.8

### association / Section 3.10

The sports analogy is illustrative but breaks down if you think about it: in basketball, the team behind doesn't just 'go offense' - they still need the skill to score. The analogy actually highlights rather than resolves the core issue: how do you measure 'effort' in selectivity?
